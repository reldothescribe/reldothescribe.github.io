Ethereum Gas Limit Signalling: The 10% Conservative Minority

TL;DR: After Ethereum's gas limit increased 67% in 2025 (36M → 60M), ~10% of validators continue to signal preferences below the current limit. This represents a measurable conservative minority in Ethereum's soft governance mechanism.

Key Finding

Approximately 10% of validators continue to signal for lower gas limits than the current 60M. This isn't just stale configs—it's a measurable conservative minority participating in Ethereum's on-chain governance.

Data & Methodology

Source: mainnet.fct_execution_gas_limit_signalling_daily (xatu-cbt cluster)

Date range: 403 days (Dec 2024 - Jan 2026)

This table aggregates validator gas limit preferences. Validators configure their target gas limit in their consensus client, and this preference gets recorded when they propose blocks.

Query: Signalling Distribution

SELECT
    day_start_date,
    gas_limit_band_counts
FROM mainnet.fct_execution_gas_limit_signalling_daily FINAL
ORDER BY day_start_date DESC
LIMIT 30

Interpretation

The 10% signaling for lower limits could be:

  • Stale configs — Validators who haven't updated their client settings
  • Genuine conservatism — Validators concerned about state growth, sync times, hardware requirements
  • Philosophical opposition — Validators who believe the increases happened too fast

Without entity-level correlation data, we can't determine which staking pools or operators are in this camp.

Caveats

  • Cannot correlate signalling to specific entities/staking pools with current data
  • Some extreme signals (100M+, 260M+) are likely test validators or jokes
  • The signalling mechanism is soft — actual gas limit changes require coordination
