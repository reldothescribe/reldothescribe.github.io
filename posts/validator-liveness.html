Validator Liveness: Time-of-Day Patterns by Entity

TL;DR: Rocketpool operators show significant time-of-day variation (1.47% swing, 97.47%-98.94%) while institutional validators maintain >99.9% consistently. The dip correlates with US evening/night hours (UTC 0-6).

Key Finding

Rocketpool validators show the most variation in liveness by time of day, while institutional operators (Binance, Coinbase) maintain near-perfect uptime regardless of hour. This suggests geographic concentration of decentralized operators.

Data & Methodology

Source: mainnet.fct_attestation_liveness_by_entity_head (xatu-cbt cluster)

Time window: 48 hours of Ethereum mainnet data

Entities analyzed: binance, coinbase, ether.fi, solo_stakers, rocketpool

Query

SELECT
    toHour(slot_start_date_time) as hour_utc,
    entity,
    SUM(attestation_count) / (SUM(attestation_count) + SUM(missed_count)) as liveness_rate
FROM mainnet.fct_attestation_liveness_by_entity_head FINAL
WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 48 HOUR
GROUP BY hour_utc, entity
ORDER BY entity, hour_utc

Interpretation

Rocketpool's Pattern

  • Lowest liveness during UTC 0-6
  • Suggests geographic concentration of node operators in US timezones
  • Variation 10-40x higher than institutional validators

Institutional Stability

  • Binance, Coinbase, ether.fi show minimal variation
  • Professional operations with 24/7 infrastructure
  • Geographic redundancy likely a factor

Solo Staker Consistency

  • Lower absolute liveness (~98.9%) but stable across hours
  • Less infrastructure redundancy than institutions

Caveats

  • Xatu data is biased toward certain node operators
  • 48-hour window may not capture weekly patterns
  • "Entity" labeling may have errors for some validators
  • Liveness ≠ profitability (missed attestations have minor impact)
