Geographic Propagation: Europe 15% Faster Than Africa

TL;DR: Blocks arrive ~300ms faster to European nodes than APAC. There's a ~200ms penalty per hop from EU origin. Finland leads at 1,744ms median arrival thanks to ethpandaops infrastructure.

The Question

How quickly do blocks propagate across different geographic regions, and what penalty do operators pay for being far from Europe?

Data Source

  • Dataset: xatu ClickHouse (mainnet.fct_block_first_seen_by_node)
  • Time range: 24 hours (ending 2026-01-31 ~12:50 UTC)
  • Observations: ~1.1 million block first-seen events
  • Countries: 30 with ≥100 observations
  • Nodes: ~180 unique nodes

Methodology

Step 1: Filter Broken Nodes

Some nodes were syncing or offline, showing arrival times of hours. Excluded nodes where:

AVG(seen_slot_start_diff) > quantile(0.50)(seen_slot_start_diff) * 10

Step 2: Geographic Comparison Query

SELECT
    meta_client_geo_country AS country,
    meta_client_geo_continent_code AS continent,
    quantile(0.50)(seen_slot_start_diff) AS p50_ms,
    quantile(0.95)(seen_slot_start_diff) AS p95_ms,
    COUNT() AS observations,
    COUNT(DISTINCT node_id) AS nodes
FROM mainnet.fct_block_first_seen_by_node FINAL
WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 24 HOUR
    AND meta_client_geo_country != ''
    AND node_id NOT IN (/* broken nodes subquery */)
GROUP BY country, continent
HAVING observations >= 1000
ORDER BY p50_ms ASC

Observations

  • Finland's lead is infrastructure-driven. ethpandaops runs significant monitoring infrastructure in Finland, which sees blocks first.
  • The ~300ms penalty for Australia/South America/Africa is pure physics. Light-speed latency from Europe to Sydney is ~100ms one-way. Factor in multiple gossip hops and the numbers check out.
  • Broken node artifacts are dramatic. One Australian node was offline, then synced 7 hours of blocks at once. This pushed Australia's "average" to 467 seconds while the median was normal.
  • The network is healthy. P95 latency globally is under 3.5 seconds. Most of the world sees blocks within 2 seconds of slot start.

Time-of-Day Patterns

No significant variation by hour. Propagation latency is stable across the day:

  • Europe p50 ranges from 1,692ms to 1,939ms (14.6% variation)
  • Variation appears random, not correlated with any time zone's business hours
