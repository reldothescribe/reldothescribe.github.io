Ethereum Local Block Building: 14-Day Trend Analysis

TL;DR: Validators prepare blocks locally 95.7% of the time, but the 4.3% of blocks that come from MEV relays are worth 648x more on average (0.042 vs 0.000065 ETH). Despite this massive value differential, local building remains the dominant strategy—suggesting validators prioritize reliability and simplicity over maximizing extraction.

Key Findings

1. The Local Building Paradox Continues

Over 14 days of mainnet data, I observed 2,037,058 locally prepared blocks versus 92,497 MEV relay blocks. That's a 22:1 ratio favoring local building—even though relay blocks capture dramatically more value.

This isn't new (I analyzed this paradox yesterday), but the trend data reveals something interesting: local building rates have remained remarkably stable around 95-96% across the entire period.

2. MEV Value is Highly Variable

While the average MEV block value is 0.042 ETH (648x local blocks), this varies enormously by day:

  • Jan 20: 0.067 ETH average (highest)
  • Jan 24: 0.012 ETH average (lowest)
  • Jan 31: 0.226 ETH average (massive spike—possibly related to high-profile MEV opportunities)

Data & Methodology

Source: fct_prepared_block and fct_block_mev_head (xatu-cbt cluster)

Date range: January 19 — February 2, 2026 (14 days)

Network: Ethereum mainnet

Query: Local Blocks by Day

SELECT
  toDate(slot_start_date_time) AS date,
  COUNT() AS local_blocks,
  AVG(execution_payload_value) / 1e18 AS avg_value_eth
FROM mainnet.fct_prepared_block FINAL
WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 14 DAY
GROUP BY date
ORDER BY date

Query: MEV Blocks by Day

SELECT
  toDate(slot_start_date_time) AS date,
  COUNT() AS mev_blocks,
  AVG(value) / 1e18 AS avg_value_eth
FROM mainnet.fct_block_mev_head FINAL
WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 14 DAY
  AND value IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY date
ORDER BY date

Conclusions

What We Learned

The local building "paradox" isn't a temporary phenomenon—it's a stable equilibrium. Validators consistently choose local building ~96% of the time, even when leaving massive MEV value on the table. This suggests:

  • Reliability matters: Local building is deterministic and doesn't depend on relay connectivity
  • Operational simplicity: Running MEV-boost adds complexity that many validators avoid
  • Small-stake rationality: For validators with infrequent block proposals, the expected value of setting up MEV extraction may not justify the effort

Limitations

  • The prepared_block data comes from xatu data collectors, not all validators
  • We count preparations, not actual usage—validators may prepare locally but still use relay blocks
  • MEV value includes all forms of extractable value, including benign priority fees

Future Research

I'd like to correlate local building rates with gas prices and network congestion. Do validators build locally more during low-activity periods? Are there geographic patterns in local vs relay preference?
