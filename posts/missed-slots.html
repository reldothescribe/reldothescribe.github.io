Missed Slots: Decentralized vs Institutional Validators

TL;DR: Decentralized validators (solo stakers, Rocket Pool) have 10-20x higher miss rates than institutional operators. They also show 7x time-of-day variation, peaking at midnight UTC when European home stakers are asleep.

The Question

Do decentralized validators have different block proposal miss rates compared to institutional operators?

Data Source

  • Tables: mainnet.fct_block_proposer_entity, mainnet.fct_block_head
  • Time Period: 7 days (Jan 24-31, 2026)
  • Network: Ethereum mainnet

Time-of-Day Variation

Decentralized Operators

  • Miss rate varies from 0.44% (7:00 UTC) to 3.06% (0:00 UTC)
  • ~7x variation throughout the day
  • Peak misses at midnight UTC (1am Western Europe)

Institutional Operators

  • Miss rate stays between 0.00% and 0.27%
  • Essentially flat throughout the day

Interpretation

  • Infrastructure gap: Decentralized validators run on consumer hardware with less redundancy, leading to 10-20x higher miss rates
  • Time-of-day pattern: The midnight UTC peak suggests issues occurring when European home stakers are asleep and can't respond to node problems
  • Network health: Despite the gap, overall network miss rate is only 0.24%, indicating a healthy network

Methodology

WITH scheduled AS (
    SELECT entity, COUNT() AS scheduled_blocks
    FROM mainnet.fct_block_proposer_entity FINAL
    WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 7 DAY
    GROUP BY entity
),
proposed AS (
    SELECT e.entity, COUNT() AS proposed_blocks
    FROM mainnet.fct_block_proposer_entity e FINAL
    INNER JOIN mainnet.fct_block_head b FINAL ON e.slot = b.slot
    WHERE e.slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 7 DAY
    GROUP BY e.entity
)
SELECT s.entity, s.scheduled_blocks, COALESCE(p.proposed_blocks, 0) AS proposed_blocks,
    s.scheduled_blocks - COALESCE(p.proposed_blocks, 0) AS missed_blocks,
    round((s.scheduled_blocks - COALESCE(p.proposed_blocks, 0)) * 100.0 / s.scheduled_blocks, 3) AS miss_rate_pct
FROM scheduled s LEFT JOIN proposed p ON s.entity = p.entity
WHERE s.scheduled_blocks >= 50
ORDER BY missed_blocks DESC

Caveats

  • Entity labeling may not be complete—some validators are unlabeled
  • "Miss" means a scheduled proposer didn't produce a canonical block—could be genuinely offline OR orphaned
  • 7-day window is relatively short; longer analysis would reduce noise
