The Home Staking Performance Gap: 83% of Missed Attestations Come From Home Operators

TL;DR: Home stakers (solo, Rocketpool, Dappnode, Bloxstaking) account for 83% of all missed Ethereum attestations. The performance gap is striking: Kraken misses 0.0004% while Bloxstaking misses 4.3%, a 10,000x difference. Rocketpool had a significant incident on Feb 1 with miss rates spiking to 4-6.7%.

The Rocketpool Feb 1 Incident

Rocketpool experienced a significant degradation on February 1, 2026. Their typical miss rate of 1.2-1.5% spiked to 4% for the entire day, with peaks hitting 6.7% during European afternoon hours (13:00-16:00 UTC).

Notably, solo stakers did NOT see a similar spike on Feb 1, suggesting this was a Rocketpool-specific issue rather than broader network stress. By 23:00 UTC, rates had recovered to near-normal levels.

Why Does This Happen?

The performance gap comes down to infrastructure:

  • Network reliability: Home internet has outages, latency spikes, and ISP maintenance windows. Datacenter connections have redundant paths and SLAs.
  • Hardware reliability: Consumer NUCs and NAS devices aren't designed for 24/7 critical workloads. Server hardware has ECC RAM, redundant power, and monitoring.
  • Operational expertise: Professional operators have on-call staff, automated failover, and incident response procedures.
  • Geographic distribution: Large operators run validators across multiple regions. Home stakers are single points of failure.

What This Means

This isn't an argument against home staking. Decentralization is essential for Ethereum's credible neutrality, and home stakers provide that even if they're less reliable individually.

But home stakers should understand the tradeoff: you're likely losing 1-4% of attestation rewards compared to professional operators. For most home stakers, the decentralization benefits outweigh this cost. But go in with open eyes.

Bloxstaking's 4.3% miss rate is particularly concerning. That's significant APR drag, and their users should know about it.

Methodology

Source: mainnet.fct_attestation_liveness_by_entity_head table (xatu-cbt cluster)

Date range: 2026-01-27 to 2026-02-03 (7 days)

Total data: 2.57M missed attestations, ~500M total attestation duties

Entity Classification

Entities are classified by the xatu pipeline based on deposit addresses, withdrawal credentials, and known staking pools. "Unknown" entities are validators that couldn't be attributed to a known pool or operator.

Query

SELECT
  entity,
  SUM(attestation_count) AS total_attestations,
  SUM(missed_count) AS total_missed,
  SUM(attestation_count) + SUM(missed_count) AS total_duties,
  SUM(attestation_count) * 100.0 / 
    (SUM(attestation_count) + SUM(missed_count)) AS liveness_pct
FROM mainnet.fct_attestation_liveness_by_entity_head FINAL
WHERE slot_start_date_time >= now() - INTERVAL 7 DAY
GROUP BY entity
ORDER BY total_duties DESC

Limitations

  • Entity attribution isn't perfect. Some validators labeled "unknown" may belong to known pools.
  • 7-day window may not be representative of long-term performance.
  • Rocketpool Feb 1 root cause unknown. Could have been infrastructure issue, software bug, or external factor.
  • Miss rate differences include both "missed" (never attested) and late attestations included in later blocks.
